# EOS Skill: Dimension Ambiguity Resolution (Nothingness Mechanic)

**Trigger:** Any probing context where a response on a dimension is ambiguous — not clearly surface (generic) and not clearly context (specific lived experience).
**Source:** Rose conversation nothingness framework, corrected by DT 2026-03-08.
**Kernel rules in play:** Rule 2 (Context Match Input Standard — input quality ceiling), Rule 8 (Operational Empathy — context-level probe, trajectory depth probe).

---

## PRINCIPLE

When a response is ambiguous on a dimension, do NOT re-ask the same question harder. Stay on the dimension. Get specificity. But get it by creating conditions for the answer to surface, not by repeating the probe with more pressure.

The trajectory must be validated or closed on the current dimension before advancing. No skipping. No parking ambiguous dimensions for later.

**Extraction kills signal. Invitation creates it.**

When you re-ask the same question with different words, the other person hears "wrong answer, try again." When you ask a different question on the same dimension that opens a new angle, their own experience creates the opening.

---

## MECHANIC

### Step 1: Detect ambiguity
The response contains some domain-relevant language but lacks the specificity of context-level input. Could be someone who has adjacent experience, or someone who knows the vocabulary but hasn't lived it. Cannot be classified as surface (too specific) or context (not specific enough).

### Step 2: Do NOT re-ask
Re-asking is the same question wearing different clothes:
- Original: "How do you handle competing priorities?"
- Re-ask: "Can you tell me more about your prioritization approach?"
- Re-ask: "What's your framework for deciding what to work on?"

These produce the same answer. The person already heard the question.

### Step 3: New-angle question on the SAME dimension
Ask a different question that opens a new angle on the same territory:
- Original: "How do you handle competing priorities?"
- New angle: "Walk me through your Monday morning. You open your laptop — what do you look at first and why?"
- New angle: "Last time something urgent landed on your desk when you were already overloaded — what happened?"

These create a specific situation where the person's actual approach reveals itself without being asked to describe it abstractly.

### Step 4: Classify and resolve
- New-angle response is context-level → ambiguity resolved, advance normally.
- New-angle response is still surface or ambiguous → dimension resolved at current depth, close it.

Ambiguous is not a final state. It resolves to surface or context after the new-angle probe. One new-angle attempt per probe step. Then close.

---

## WHY THIS WORKS

A person who has done the work will show it when placed in a concrete situation. A person who hasn't will give the same vague answer regardless of framing. The new-angle question is a diagnostic: it distinguishes "can't articulate but has the experience" from "doesn't have the experience."

The mechanic is not about patience or gentleness. It's about signal quality. Re-asking extracts compliance. New angles extract truth.

---

## SCOPE

Applies whenever EOS is probing for depth on a dimension:
- Rule 8 trajectory depth probes during any project conversation
- Candidate evaluation conversations (product-specific implementation handled separately)
- Any context where input quality on a dimension determines CCI or scoring

Does NOT apply to:
- Factual questions with verifiable answers (re-ask directly)
- Goal clarification (Rule 1 — ambiguity means push for specificity, not new-angle)
- Builder Mode (Module D — no clarifying questions unless genuine blocker)
